The world now recognizes China as a superpower. China's influence and overall national strength in the United Nations and the world still lag far behind the United States. In 2020, China faces the impact of COVID-19 and the trade war at the same time and still stands tall in its economic position. Maybe many people will ask why China can still maintain a strong position in the world. There is only one answer: China successfully joined the WTO in 2001.

China's reform and opening for more than 30 years. Economic development has achieved world-renowned achievements. In about 2000, China was the world's sixth-largest economy, and its surpassed Germany to become the world's third economy in 2007. When will China's economic scale surpass the United States has been a topic of concern to economists in recent years. "Surpassing Britain and catching up with the United States" is the dream of the Chinese people. Now, China is now the second-largest economy in the world.

When talking about why joining the WTO is the answer, let’s take a look at how China joined the WTO.

After China’s accession to the WTO, it will regulate market economic order, reduce nominal tariffs, abolish preferential policies, implement national treatment, and strengthen policy transparency in accordance with international rules.

In line with the WTO's free trade spirit, China promised the international community to substantially reduce tariffs, relax import licenses, and substantially liberalize the domestic commodity market. After joining the WTO, with the reduction of tariff rates, the price of imported goods will fall on the premise of maintaining the RMB exchange rate's stability, which will benefit the growth of imports.


China's foreign exchange reserves have grown rapidly after joining the WTO. The more obvious increase is from 2003 to 2007. China's foreign exchange reserves have grown by as much as 280% in five years. China relaxes market access restrictions for foreign capital entering the mainland System, directly stimulating foreign capital investment in the mainland. When China’s foreign exchange reserves increase, it means that foreign investment in China has been increasing. This will form a new force to assist China’s economic recovery.


The number of exported and imported goods has also been increased overall. According to the WTO report, China’s imports and exports ranked seventh in the world in 2000. A few years after joining the WTO, China’s import and export volume reached third place in the world in 2006. China has officially become a major trading country.

Why China?

In 2001, Workers in cities and towns can get an annual salary of 25,068 RMB per year. Their monthly salary is about 2089 RMB. For reference, the monthly salary in Hong Kong at that time was about 11000 HKD per month in 2001. Hiring one person in a more developed city is equivalent to hiring four people in China.


China has the largest workforce in the world. The global working population is approximately 2800 million people in 2001, of which China’s working population is 727 million people. China’s working population already accounted for about 25% of the world. The WTO has brought China's advantages to the world. With a cheap and huge labor force, it will naturally attract foreign investment to China for development. The expansion of foreign capital investment in China can increase employment opportunities, thereby increasing residents' income and consumption capacity. On the other hand, it will also affect driving China’s domestic investment due to supporting needs, which is conducive to improving the economy.

The evolution of China's industry

After China accedes to the WTO, it will attract more international capital, introduce foreign advanced technology and equipment, and urge the domestic production factor market to integrate with the international market. Various industries will participate in the international labor division through the global production factor flow, conducive to domestic resources' configuration.

China's industry began to develop into the secondary and tertiary industries. Due to the entry of many foreign companies, the number of manufacturing industries has grown rapidly after 2001. The secondary industry proportion increased from 49.4% in 2001 to 57.9% in 2002, an increase of 8.5%. The subsequent changes are not big after 2002. The proportion of tertiary industry has also begun to increase after 2002.

China has begun to industrialize and modernize under the trend of the world.

China ushered in a new era after joining the WTO. The combination of capitalism and communism formally brought China into the world’s track. In the long run, China’s accession to the WTO was a decision without any disadvantage. This decision became the starting point for China to go to the world's second economy.
